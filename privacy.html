Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 28, 2026

Overview

Very Wild Jacks ("we", "our", "the game") is a free-to-play online board game. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what information we collect and how we use it.

Information We Collect

We collect minimal information to provide the game experience:

  • Player Name: The display name you enter is stored locally on your device using browser localStorage. It is shared with other players in your game session only.
  • Game Session Data: Room IDs and game state are stored temporarily in your browser's localStorage to support reconnection. This data is not sent to any server beyond what is needed for peer-to-peer connections.
  • Connection Data: We use PeerJS for peer-to-peer connections. PeerJS may collect connection metadata (such as IP addresses) as part of establishing WebRTC connections. Please refer to the PeerJS documentation for their privacy practices.

Cookies & Local Storage

We use browser localStorage to save your player name and game session information. We do not use tracking cookies. Third-party advertising partners (Google AdSense) may use cookies to serve relevant ads.

Third-Party Services

  • Google AdSense: We use Google AdSense to display advertisements. Google may use cookies and web beacons to serve ads based on your browsing activity. You can learn more at Google's Privacy Policy.
  • PeerJS: Used for peer-to-peer game connections. See PeerJS for details.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or share your personal information with third parties. Your player name is only visible to other players in your active game session.

Children's Privacy

This game is suitable for all ages. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information, please contact us.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.

Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, please reach out via our GitHub repository.
